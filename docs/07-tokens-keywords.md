## Tokens & Keywords

These are some keywords and literals you may find in Lunite.  

### Keywords

- `let`, `const`, `func`, `class`, `enum`, `global`, `public`, `private`, `async`, `await`, `macro`
- `if`, `else`, `while`, `for`, `in`, `match`, `other`
- `attempt`, `rescue`, `finally`, `assert`, `return`, `break`, `advance`, `leap`
- `new`, `import`, `import_py`, `from`
- `and`, `or`, `not`, `is`

### Literals

- Numbers: `123`, `3.14`
- Char: `'a'` (exactly one character in single quotes)
- String: `"hello"`, (supports escapes like `\n`, `\t`, `\\`, `\"`, `\uXXXX`, must use double quotes)
- F-string: `f"Hello {name}"` (expressions inside `{}` are evaluated like in Python)
- List: `[1, 2, 3]` (iterable list)
- Dict: `{ "key": 10, "other": 20 }` (key-value pair lists)
- Set: `{ a, b, c}` (dict with no keys and no duplicate elements)
- Tuple: `(1, 2, 3)` and single-parenthesized value `(x)`
- Booleans: `true`, `false` (represent off/on or 0/1)
- Null: `null` (equivalent to Java's null or Python's None)
